About Haruna

I am a seasoned Network, Systems, and HPC Engineer with a proven track record in designing, automating, and managing large-scale on-premises and cloud infrastructures. I hold a PhD from the University of Glasgow, with a research focus on Network Programmability, Edge/Cloud Computing, and Network Function Virtualization (NFV).

My expertise spans Infrastructure as Code (IaC) using Terraform, automation with Ansible, datacentre networking and server administration, high-speed 200Gbps ethernet networking, Lustre storage, CEPH storage, as well as deployment and management of public and private clouds, including OpenStack. I also lead strategic IT planning initiatives, helping organizations align infrastructure with business goals.

In addition to my technical skills, I am a passionate educator and researcher, with over 10 years of experience teaching Computing Science courses at the university level. I have published several research papers in top-tier conferences and journals, and I am a regular reviewer for IEEE and ACM conferences.

I am currently a High-Performance Computing (HPC) Systems Engineer at the University of Sussex, UK, where I am responsible for designing, deploying, and managing the university's HPC infrastructure. I am also a Researcher and Tutor at the University of Glasgow, where I teach Linux and HPC courses and conduct research in Network Programmability and NFV.

I am always looking for new opportunities to collaborate on exciting projects, so please feel free to reach out if you would like to work together!
